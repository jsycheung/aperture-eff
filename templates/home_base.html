The aperture efficiency is given by $$A_{eff} = \eta_a \frac{\pi}{4} D^2$$
$$\eta_a = \frac{3520}{D^2}\frac{T_a^\prime}{S_\nu}$$
$$T_a^\prime = T_a e^{\tau_0 A}$$ where \(A\) is airmass towards which the measurement is made (assuming elevation is larger than \(15^\circ, A = \frac{1}{\sin(\text{elevation (arcsec)})}\)), \(\tau_0\) is the atmospheric optical depth at the zenith assumed to be 0.01, \(T_a\) is the observed source antenna temperature in Kelvin, \(T_a^\prime\) is the corrected antenna temperature in Kelvin, \(D\) is the diameter in meters, and \(S_\nu\) is the spectral flux density in Jansky.

The spectral flux density for each source is frequency dependent, it was obtained by fitting the data with a polynomial function of the form $$\log S = a_0 + a_1 \log (\nu_G) + a_2 [\log (\nu_G)]^2 + a_3 [\log (\nu_G)]^3 + \cdots$$ The fitted coefficients for the 20 sources are as follows:
